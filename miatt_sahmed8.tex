\begin{abstract}
\noindent
We build and evaluate a six-approach multi-site brain MRI landmark detection pipeline
predicting 51 anatomical landmarks across six simulated acquisition sites (642 labeled,
162 unlabeled subjects).
A 3D CNN achieves the best internal accuracy at \textbf{4.54\,mm} mean Euclidean error
on a 20\% per-site holdout and is submitted for all 162 unlabeled subjects.
Key findings: rigid ACPC-space registration is algebraically equivalent to the mean
baseline; posterior-weighted centroids add noise for sub-structure boundaries; coordinate
normalisation is critical for regression with targets far from the origin; orbital landmarks
(LE, RE) remain the dominant error source at 11--13\,mm across all approaches.
\end{abstract}

\section{Site Characteristics}
\label{sec:sites}

Six sites provide 107--108 labeled and 27 unlabeled subjects each (siteB: 106 labeled).
Per subject: T1 NIfTI, T2, six tissue posterior maps (WM, GM, CSF, Ventricle Bodies,
Globus Pallidus, Background); labeled subjects also have 51 ground-truth landmarks in
physical space (mm).

\textbf{Orientation.}
Calling \texttt{SimpleITK.GetDirection()} on all 642 T1 volumes returns the identity matrix,
confirming images are axis-aligned in SimpleITK's internal LPS convention — consistent with
what the EDA HTML reports show.
There are no rotational mismatches between or within sites;
the physical-to-voxel mapping reduces to translate-and-scale.
(The FCSV landmark files use RAS convention, so $x$ and $y$ sign-flips are required when
calling \texttt{TransformPoint}; see Section~\ref{sec:coords}.)

\textbf{Spacing.}
Constant within each site (std $= 0$); ranges $\approx 0.92$--$1.10$\,mm across sites.
See Appendix~\ref{app:eda} for per-site values.

\textbf{Physical origin and AC position.}
AC is always at $(0,0,0)$\,mm for siteA (pre-ACPC-aligned), but varies by
$\sigma\approx17$\,mm per axis for sites B--F (native scanner space, purely translational
scatter — no rotation).
The principal challenge of cross-site generalisation is therefore translational offset and
mild spacing variation, not arbitrary orientation.
The AC--PC distance is $27\,\text{mm}$ ($\sigma \approx 1\,\text{mm}$) across all sites.

\section{Design Decisions}
\label{sec:design}

All approaches use a deterministic 80/20 per-site labeled split ($\approx$21 eval subjects
per site) and report mean Euclidean error over all 51 landmarks.
Table~\ref{tab:results_summary} summarises results; full per-site breakdowns are in
Appendix~\ref{app:results}.

\textbf{Approach 1 — Per-site mean in ACPC space (baseline, 4.59\,mm).}
Each subject's landmarks are transformed to ACPC space, averaged, then mapped back to
scanner space via the target subject's ACPC transform.
Averaging must occur in ACPC space — scanner-space averaging would fail for sites B--F due to
the $\sigma\approx17$\,mm AC scatter.
RE (12.4\,mm) and LE (11.4\,mm) dominate error; AC and PC are near-perfect by construction.

\textbf{Approach 2 — Rigid registration to ACPC template (null result, 4.59\,mm).}
A mean T1 ACPC template is built per site; unlabeled subjects' T1s are rigidly registered
(6\,DOF, Mattes MI) to propagate the mean landmark set.
This matches Approach~1 exactly: the registration refines the ACPC transform estimate, but
the propagated landmarks remain the population mean.
Rigid registration of a mean cannot reduce anatomical variation around that mean; improvement
requires subject-specific image-content learning.
The per-subject approximate ACPC transform $T_\text{approx}$ produced here is reused by
Approaches 3, 4, and 6.

\textbf{Approach 3 — Posterior-guided local refinement (negative result, 5.13\,mm, siteA only).}
For eight CC sub-structure landmarks, the mean prediction was replaced with a
WM-posterior-weighted centroid within an 8\,mm sphere.
Score regressed by $+$0.54\,mm (genu $+$4.42\,mm, rostrum $+$4.62\,mm) because
$P_\text{WM}$ is high throughout all white matter, not specifically at CC boundaries.
Posterior-weighted centroids are only valid when the landmark coincides with the tissue
centre of mass (e.g., ventricular body).

\textbf{Approach 4 — 3D CNN coordinate regression (best, 4.54\,mm, submitted).}
A 5-block stride-2 encoder CNN ($1{\to}24{\to}48{\to}96{\to}192{\to}384$ channels)
followed by global average pooling and two FC layers ($384{\to}256{\to}153$, Tanh output)
predicts all 51 landmarks from the ACPC-resampled T1 ($96{\times}101{\times}101$, 2\,mm).
Trained cross-site ($\approx$516 subjects), 100 epochs, AdamW + cosine LR, smooth-L1 loss.
\emph{Critical fix:} the first run (no normalisation) yielded 14.1\,mm; normalising targets
by 120\,mm to $[-1,+1]$ consistent with Tanh reduced this to \textbf{4.54\,mm}.
Without normalisation, a zero-initialised regression head predicts all landmarks near the
origin — correct for AC but catastrophic for peripheral landmarks at $\pm100$\,mm.
Cross-site pooling is natural because ACPC space places all subjects in the same frame.

\textbf{Approach 5 — Multi-atlas affine registration (5.51\,mm).}
Five siteA atlases registered (12\,DOF, Mattes MI) to each target; landmarks transferred
and fused by median per axis.
Worse than baseline: affine registration in scanner space is destabilised by the B--F
translational scatter, and imperfect registrations add noise to the median vote.

\textbf{Approach 6 — BCD-inspired LLS from tissue posteriors (4.97\,mm).}
Re-implements BRAINSConstellationDetector's \cite{johnson2007brainsfit} LLS step in Python.
Six posteriors sampled at the 51 mean ACPC positions projected into scanner space yield a
306-dimensional feature vector; ridge regression ($\alpha=10$) maps features to ACPC-space
coordinates.
Matches CNN on siteA (4.38\,mm, where $T_\text{approx}$ is exact) but degrades for B--F
($\sim$5\,mm) where approximate ACPC transforms introduce systematic sampling errors.

\section{Output Coordinate Space and the AC\,=\,(0,0,0) Pitfall}
\label{sec:coords}

External evaluation compares predicted landmarks to withheld ground-truth in \emph{scanner
space} (native T1 coordinates).
A naive implementation saves predictions in ACPC space, where AC is forced to
$(0,0,0)$\,mm by construction — correct for siteA (which is already ACPC-aligned) but
wrong by $\sim$40\,mm for sites B--F where the physical AC lies far from the origin.

\textbf{Initial (incorrect) output.}
All 162 unlabeled predictions were saved in ACPC space.
For siteA, errors were small ($\sim$4.5\,mm) because ACPC $\approx$ scanner space.
For siteB--F, the AC error alone was $\sim$40\,mm; overall errors exceeded 45\,mm.

\textbf{After the fix.}
Each unlabeled subject's predictions are converted to scanner space using
$T_\text{approx}^{-1}$ (the inverse of the approximate ACPC transform from Approach~2).
A secondary bug was that \texttt{SimpleITK.TransformPoint} operates in LPS convention while
FCSV files use RAS, requiring sign-flips on $x$ and $y$ before and after the call.
After both fixes, siteB unlabeled AC reads $(-33.4,\,-6.3,\,-5.3)\,\text{mm}$ — consistent
with the observed siteB AC scatter of $\sigma\approx17$\,mm.
Estimated scanner-space error for unlabeled subjects is $\approx$9.3\,mm overall
(vs.\ 4.54\,mm on labeled holdout where $T_\text{true}$ is available).

\section{Verification and Reproducibility}
\label{sec:verif}

\textbf{Internal evaluation} uses a deterministic 20\% per-site holdout.
\textbf{Geometric verification} checks AC at $(0,0,0)$, $|\text{AC}_{SI}-\text{PC}_{SI}|<0.1\,\text{mm}$,
$|\text{AC}_{LR}-\text{PC}_{LR}|<0.1\,\text{mm}$, and $|\text{LE}_{SI}-\text{RE}_{SI}|<0.1\,\text{mm}$
for all labeled training subjects (HTML report: \texttt{eda\_report\_acpc\_verification.html}).
\textbf{Test suite:} 40 unit tests covering ACPC transform, error metrics, and FCSV I/O
pass under \texttt{pixi run test}.

\textbf{Reproducibility.}
A new user on the MIATT workstation in July 2026: clone the repository, run
\texttt{pixi install} ($\approx$5\,min, reads \texttt{pixi.lock} — fully pinned), verify with
\texttt{pixi run test}, then \texttt{pixi run pipeline --approach cnn --output predictions}.
The CNN checkpoint, ACPC templates, and per-subject ACPC caches are generated on first run
and reused thereafter; re-runs complete in under five minutes.
No site-specific configuration is required.

% ---------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Landmark Selection for Non-linear Registration}
\label{sec:landmark_selection}

Mislocalised landmarks can invert a non-linear registration locally \cite{rohlfing2012image}.
Based on CNN errors (Table~\ref{tab:landmark_errors}, Appendix~\ref{app:landmarks}):
\textbf{exclude} all 15 with mean error $>5$\,mm (RE 12.6, LE 11.2, cortical poles/sulcal extremes
6--9\,mm, lateral inner ear 7--8\,mm, dens\_axis 5.7\,mm — orbital regions or cortical surfaces,
low T1 contrast);
\textbf{hard constraints} — the 26 with error $\leq4$\,mm (AC, PC, corpus callosum family,
caudate heads, lateral ventricles, optic chiasm, BPons, SMV, CM — distinct anatomy spanning all
three axes);
\textbf{soft constraints with reduced weight} — the 10 at 4--5\,mm
(l/r\_corp, l/r\_prim\_ext, l\_temp\_pole, mid\_prim\_sup).

\textbf{Conclusion.}
The 3D CNN achieves \textbf{4.54\,mm} on the internal holdout and is submitted.
LE and RE (11--13\,mm) are the dominant bottleneck; adding T2 as a second input channel is the
highest-leverage future improvement.
The pipeline requires zero code changes for a new site by design.
